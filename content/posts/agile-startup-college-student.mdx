# What It's Like to Work in an Agile Startup as a Full-Time College Student

Most people picture a COO as someone in a corner office managing a team of fifty. At GreenMark, it looks a little different. I am a full-time college student, and I help run a three-person startup focused on LEED certification and ESG initiatives in commercial real estate. No corner office. Just a lot of coordination, a lot of structure, and a whole lot of moving parts.

## The Role Nobody Talks About

The COO role is easy to overlook from the outside. You do not get the glory. You are in the back end, behind the scenes, making sure everything actually moves. I like to think of myself as the glue and the clarity.

My CEO brings the vision and stays deep in the industry. My CTO builds. My job is everything in between. I take the ideas from my CEO, structure them into actionable plans, and hand them off in a way that my CTO can actually execute. I keep the source of truth of the business. I finalize documentation on where we are, what we are focused on, and where we are going. I handle finances, accounting, and revenue generation. I also decide when we need to slow down and when we need to speed up.

It sounds like a lot because it is. But it is also the role I am most built for as a business major. While my CEO gets deep in the industry and my CTO focuses on building, I get to focus on the business itself.

## What Agile Actually Means for a Team of Three

Agile for us is not a buzzword. It means one goal at a time, with real dates and real checkpoints. We lock in on that goal, work toward the outcome, and let that outcome lead us to the next one. My job is to define those goals and keep the team focused on them.

I built an agile workflow that pulls from all of our meeting transcripts and ideas, structures them, and uses AI to filter what actually makes sense given GreenMark's current state. I trained the model on our context, our meetings with prospects, and outside research so it can help me make educated decisions with real backed data. It has become the best brainstorming tool I have. Every day it helps me uncover something new.

I also built AI agents to assist with lead outreach. They pull from a database of prospects, research each person, and present me with what to know before I reach out. From there I write a personalized email myself, genuinely interested in how I can help them. It keeps the process efficient without losing the human side of it.

## Keeping the Boat Afloat

One of the hardest parts of this role is knowing when to hold the line. This past week we had a meeting with someone in commercial real estate who gave us a perspective that could have easily sent us in a different direction. The finance side of real estate is a real market. But that is not our focus right now. GreenMark is built around LEED certification and ESG initiatives, and steering away from that — even toward something promising — would mean losing the ground we have already built.

In that moment my job was to hear the feedback, take it seriously, and redirect the team back to the goal. That takes logical thinking instead of acting on emotion. When something interesting comes up, we note it, and we decide if it is something to explore later. But we do not chase it in the moment.

## Being Students First

One thing that makes this team work is that we all know our roles and we do not overstep. We delegate effectively and trust each other to handle our lanes. We are also all students, which means life gets complicated sometimes. If someone is in the middle of a rough exam week, the rest of us pick up the slack. We have all had those weeks where we needed to step back. But we never stop moving.

That balance is something I am proud of. Running a startup while managing a full course load is not easy, but it has taught me more about operations, leadership, and execution than any classroom could on its own.
