# Why Your Best Idea Is Never Your First One

When we started, we were not GreenMark. We were PropFlow.

PropFlow was built to help property managers communicate better with their tenants. It was a real idea solving a real problem. But after getting into the market and doing the research, we realized the industry was too saturated and the return was not there. So we made a change.

That was the first of many.

## The First Pivot: Following the Money

After stepping away from PropFlow we started looking at tax incentives for property owners. The idea was simple. If we could show property owners a return on their investment through incentives, we had something worth building.

Then we started talking to actual property owners.

What we found was not pleasant, but it was necessary. Property owners care about money. In a market like State College where supply is low and demand is high, a rental property fills itself. Why invest more into something that already works? The incentives were real but the value was not landing. We had our answer and we had to move again.

## Finding a Bigger Market

Getting deeper into sustainability and ESG networks opened a new door. Commercial real estate. This is where things started to click.

CRE owners think differently. They care about cutting energy costs, reducing carbon emissions, and staying compliant. That word — compliant — became important to us. After a discovery meeting with an ESG consultant we started to see the pattern. These owners care about their ESG reporting. It makes them look good, it keeps them in line with regulations, and it signals to the market that they are doing the right thing.

But underneath all of it, they were still in it for the money. And honestly, that is fair.

So our angle became this: what if we could take aggregated data across state and federal programs, compile it into one place, and show property owners exactly what renovations would get them money back? Helping with reporting, increasing property value, staying compliant. It was all starting to come together. But there were still gaps.

## Digging Deeper

Who exactly was our customer? Property owners made sense but the problem was they are not educated in LEED and ESG standards. They do not understand why it matters or see the value in it yet. Educating someone and then selling to them is a lot of friction for a startup that is just getting started. We needed to go deeper.

That is when we started looking closely at LEED itself and the USGBC. What we found was that a lot of the process for certifying these buildings is completely manual. Excel sheets, data transfers between software, fragmented communication between building owners, consultants, and engineers. It is slow, it is outdated, and nobody has fixed it.

That was it. We found the problem.

## Where We Are Now

As of right now we are in conversations with real industry leaders navigating how we can help. There is still room to niche down further and we know that. But every conversation we have sharpens the picture a little more. We have a direction we are excited about, one that sits at the intersection of sustainability, compliance, and an industry growing faster than the infrastructure around it can keep up with. We are not ready to say more than that yet.

What I can say is that GreenMark's mission is to automate sustainability at scale. Everything we have learned since December has been pointing us toward what that actually means in practice.

## The Lesson

From PropFlow to GreenMark, from residential to commercial, from tax incentives to LEED compliance — every shift felt like a step backward in the moment. It was not. Each one saved us months of building the wrong thing.

The idea is never going to be perfect. There will always be something else to uncover, someone else to talk to, a gap you did not see before. The goal is not to find the final answer. The goal is to keep iterating until you are close enough to build something real.

We are not there yet. But we are closer than we were yesterday.
